LLM Frontiers:
Advancement Meets Responsibility

A One-Day Workshop at Rice University

Department of Computer Science • Rice University • Houston, Texas

Bringing together researchers and practitioners to explore the forefront of large language model development while emphasizing the imperative of responsible AI practices.

About the Workshop

Large Language Models (LLMs) have rapidly evolved into foundational tools across a wide range of industries and research domains. With the growing scale and capabilities of models such as GPT-4, Claude, LLaMA, and Gemini, we are witnessing unprecedented advances in natural language understanding, generation, and reasoning.

At the same time, these advancements bring pressing challenges in Responsible AI, including concerns around transparency, safety, fairness, accountability, and regulatory oversight.

This workshop covers a wide range of topics centered on LLMs, from novel architectures and data curation to pre-training and post-training strategies, as well as critical themes such as interpretability, safety, efficiency, agentic AI, and human-AI collaboration.

Workshop Goals

  • Elevate Rice's research and scholarly visibility by showcasing cutting-edge work on LLMs
  • Foster interdisciplinary collaboration across computer science, engineering, data science, cognitive science, and social sciences
  • Engage Rice's institutes and centers, such as the Ken Kennedy Institute, the Baker Institute, and the Data Science Initiative, in shaping Responsible AI
  • Contribute to the broader research community by facilitating the exchange of novel ideas, tools, and best practices in LLM research and Responsible AI
  • Support student development and training through dedicated sessions, mentorship opportunities, and participation in presentations and discussions
  • Create meaningful engagement opportunities for Rice students, alumni, and the Houston community through open sessions, public panels, and educational materials
